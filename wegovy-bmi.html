What BMI Qualifies for Wegovy?

The short answer: Wegovy℠ (semaglutide 2.4 mg injection) is FDA-approved for chronic weight management in adults with a BMI of 30 or higher (obesity), or a BMI of 27 or higher with at least one weight-related comorbidity such as hypertension, type 2 diabetes, dyslipidemia, cardiovascular disease, or obstructive sleep apnea. Unlike Ozempic, Wegovy has explicit FDA-printed BMI thresholds on its label — making it the most clearly defined GLP-1 option for patients seeking weight management eligibility criteria. It is also approved for adolescents aged 12 and older with obesity.

FDA-Approved Indication and BMI Threshold for Wegovy

Wegovy℠ (semaglutide injection 2.4 mg once weekly) was first approved by the FDA in June 2021 for chronic weight management in adults. Its approved indications as of 2026 include:

  • Reduction of excess body weight and long-term maintenance in adults with obesity (BMI ≥ 30 kg/m²)
  • Reduction of excess body weight in adults who are overweight (BMI ≥ 27 kg/m²) with at least one weight-related comorbidity
  • Reduction of risk of major adverse cardiovascular events (CV death, non-fatal heart attack, non-fatal stroke) in adults with established cardiovascular disease and obesity or overweight (approved via the SELECT trial, 2023)
  • Chronic weight management in pediatric patients aged 12 and older with obesity (BMI at or above the 95th percentile for age and sex)

In March 2026, the FDA approved Wegovy HD (7.2 mg semaglutide weekly) under the Commissioner's National Priority Voucher program — the first higher-dose option for patients who need additional weight reduction beyond the standard 2.4 mg dose. Wegovy HD carries the same BMI eligibility thresholds as the standard formulation.

Key distinction: Wegovy is the only semaglutide formulation with explicit FDA-label BMI thresholds for weight management. Its sister medication Ozempic is approved for type 2 diabetes without a BMI criterion. The same active ingredient (semaglutide) in a higher weekly dose is what distinguishes Wegovy's superior weight-loss outcomes.

How BMI Is Calculated and What Your Number Means

Body Mass Index provides a standard weight-for-height screening measure used by the FDA, CDC, and the majority of insurance payers to determine GLP-1 eligibility. The formula is:

Metric: BMI = weight (kg) ÷ height (m)²

Imperial: BMI = (weight in lbs × 703) ÷ height in inches²

To illustrate: a person who is 5 feet 5 inches tall and weighs 185 lbs has a BMI of approximately 30.8 — just above the obesity threshold and clearly eligible for Wegovy without a comorbidity requirement. At 175 lbs, the same person's BMI would be 29.1 — in the overweight range, requiring a comorbidity such as hypertension or high cholesterol for Wegovy eligibility.

BMI should be understood as a screening tool rather than a complete metabolic picture. The CDC notes that BMI does not differentiate between fat mass and lean muscle mass. However, for the purpose of medication eligibility and insurance prior authorization, BMI remains the primary documented threshold. What If Your BMI Is Below the Threshold?

If your BMI is below 27, you do not meet the FDA-approved indication for Wegovy for weight management. However, several clinical pathways may still apply:

Comorbidities That Can Qualify You at BMI 27–29.9

If your BMI is in the 27–29.9 overweight range, any of the following documented comorbidities make you eligible for Wegovy under the FDA's approved labeling:

  • Hypertension — Stage 1 (systolic ≥ 130 mmHg) or Stage 2 (≥ 140 mmHg) blood pressure diagnoses are widely accepted
  • Type 2 diabetes mellitus — also opens the door to Ozempic and Mounjaro on-label
  • Dyslipidemia — elevated LDL cholesterol, elevated triglycerides, or low HDL cholesterol
  • Established cardiovascular disease — prior heart attack, stroke, angina, or peripheral artery disease
  • Obstructive sleep apnea — a diagnosis supported by a sleep study (PSG or home sleep test)

It is worth noting that NHLBI obesity risk data show that even overweight patients (BMI 27–29.9) with comorbidities carry meaningfully elevated cardiovascular and metabolic risk that GLP-1 therapy is well-positioned to address.

Step Therapy and Prior Authorization

Even when BMI thresholds are met, most commercial insurers require prior authorization for Wegovy. This process typically involves: documented BMI from a recent clinical visit, ICD-10 diagnosis codes for obesity and any comorbidities, evidence of prior attempts at lifestyle-based weight management (at minimum 6 months), and sometimes a letter of medical necessity from your provider. Some plans also require trial of lower-cost pharmacotherapy (phentermine, topiramate, or bupropion-naltrexone) before approving Wegovy.

What If Your BMI Is Above 40?

Class III obesity (BMI ≥ 40) is a condition with elevated risk across multiple organ systems. The NHLBI identifies Class III obesity as associated with dramatically elevated risks for type 2 diabetes, cardiovascular disease, obstructive sleep apnea, osteoarthritis, and several cancers. Patients with BMI ≥ 40 are well within Wegovy's approved indication and often achieve the highest absolute weight losses in clinical trials because they start at a higher baseline.

At BMI ≥ 40, it is clinically appropriate to simultaneously evaluate bariatric surgical options. Roux-en-Y gastric bypass, sleeve gastrectomy, and duodenal switch procedures can produce 25–35% total body weight loss, which may exceed what is achievable with GLP-1 therapy alone. However, many patients prefer the non-surgical route of GLP-1 therapy and can achieve excellent results. The decision is individualized and best made with an obesity medicine specialist or bariatric surgeon. Wegovy and Ozempic are sometimes used as pre-surgical weight-loss agents to reduce operative risk before bariatric procedures.

Insurance Coverage for Wegovy by BMI Bracket

Wegovy coverage is one of the most complex areas in GLP-1 therapy due to rapidly evolving payer policies. As of mid-2026:

Commercial Insurance

Most major commercial carriers (UnitedHealth, Aetna, Cigna, BCBS, Humana) cover Wegovy for members meeting FDA criteria — BMI ≥ 30 or BMI ≥ 27 with a documented comorbidity. Prior authorization is nearly universal. Required documentation typically includes: BMI from chart, ICD-10 codes (E66.x for obesity, comorbidity codes), documentation of prior diet/lifestyle attempts, and sometimes a letter of medical necessity. Coverage determinations vary by plan benefit design and employer-specific formulary decisions.

Medicare Part D

Current law (the Social Security Act) classifies weight-loss drugs as excluded from Part D coverage. However, Wegovy gained Medicare coverage for its cardiovascular risk-reduction indication in 2024 — meaning Medicare beneficiaries with established cardiovascular disease and obesity or overweight may have coverage under Part D for that specific indication. The TREAT Act and other proposed legislation, if passed, could expand Medicare coverage of Wegovy for weight management more broadly.

Medicaid

State Medicaid coverage is highly variable. As of 2026, a growing number of states have added GLP-1 weight-management medications to their formularies with prior authorization. Check your state's Medicaid drug coverage list or ask your provider for a prior authorization submission.

Real-World Expectations: How Much Weight Loss to Expect on Wegovy

Wegovy has the most robust weight-loss clinical trial dataset of any GLP-1 medication currently available, anchored by the landmark STEP 1 through STEP 5 trial series.

In STEP 1, published in the New England Journal of Medicine (2021), 1,961 adults with obesity or overweight with comorbidities (but without type 2 diabetes) taking once-weekly semaglutide 2.4 mg lost a mean of 14.9% of body weight at 68 weeks, compared to 2.4% in the placebo group. The percentage of participants achieving specific milestones: 86.4% achieved ≥5% weight loss; 69.1% achieved ≥10%; 50.5% achieved ≥15%; and 32% achieved ≥20% weight loss.

STEP 2 evaluated semaglutide 2.4 mg in adults with type 2 diabetes, showing average weight loss of 9.6% versus 3.4% with placebo — reflecting the metabolic complexity of T2D but still representing clinically meaningful improvement. STEP 5, a 2-year trial, showed that weight loss was sustained with continued treatment, with a mean reduction of 16.7% in body weight at 104 weeks.

The 2026 STEP UP trial of Wegovy HD (7.2 mg) showed approximately 20.7% mean weight loss at 72 weeks — offering an even higher-ceiling option for patients who plateau on the standard 2.4 mg dose. A higher proportion of Wegovy HD patients also achieved significant milestones: 79.8% achieved ≥10% weight loss and 63.8% achieved ≥15% weight loss, per Novo Nordisk's medlink data.

These outcomes are achieved in conjunction with a reduced-calorie diet and increased physical activity. Patients who do not maintain dietary and lifestyle changes typically see diminished results. Real-world outcomes from observational data tend to be somewhat lower than clinical trial outcomes due to variable adherence and dosing patterns.

Maintenance After Reaching Goal Weight

The clinical evidence on long-term weight maintenance with semaglutide is clear and somewhat sobering: GLP-1 therapy suppresses appetite and metabolic compensation mechanisms, but those effects require ongoing drug exposure to persist.

The STEP 1 extension study, published in Diabetes, Obesity and Metabolism (2022) by Wilding et al., followed 327 participants after withdrawal of semaglutide 2.4 mg at week 68. From week 68 to week 120 (one year post-withdrawal), participants regained an average of 11.6 percentage points of previously lost weight, resulting in a net loss from baseline of only 5.6%. Critically, cardiometabolic improvements — in blood pressure, blood sugar, waist circumference, and lipids — also largely reverted toward baseline.

A 2026 University of Cambridge modeling study in eClinicalMedicine projected that weight regain plateaus at approximately 75% of original loss by 60–80 weeks after stopping, suggesting roughly 25% of initial weight loss may be maintained long-term. The researchers emphasized that the findings reinforce the chronicity of obesity as a disease requiring ongoing management.

Long-term maintenance strategies supported by evidence include: dose optimization (including titrating to Wegovy HD if available), structured behavioral intervention with dietitian support, resistance training to preserve lean mass during weight-loss phases, and regular follow-up with an obesity medicine specialist. Some patients successfully taper to a lower dose for maintenance rather than stopping entirely, though this is an off-label approach requiring clinical guidance.

Frequently Asked Questions: Wegovy BMI Eligibility

What BMI do you need for Wegovy?

The FDA-approved BMI thresholds for Wegovy are: BMI ≥ 30 (obesity, no comorbidity required), or BMI ≥ 27 with at least one weight-related comorbidity such as hypertension, type 2 diabetes, dyslipidemia, cardiovascular disease, or obstructive sleep apnea. These thresholds are explicitly stated in the Wegovy FDA prescribing information. For pediatric patients aged 12 and older, the threshold is a BMI at or above the 95th percentile for age and sex.

How much weight can you lose on Wegovy?

Clinical trials show average weight loss of 14.9% of body weight over 68 weeks in adults without diabetes (STEP 1 trial). With the newer Wegovy HD (7.2 mg), the STEP UP trial showed approximately 20.7% average weight loss at 72 weeks. Results vary significantly by individual: 50.5% of STEP 1 participants achieved ≥15% weight loss, and 32% achieved ≥20%. Combining Wegovy with a reduced-calorie diet and regular physical activity is required for optimal outcomes.

Does insurance cover Wegovy?

Coverage varies by plan. Most major commercial insurance plans cover Wegovy for patients meeting FDA BMI criteria, but require prior authorization. Medicare Part D covers Wegovy only for its cardiovascular risk-reduction indication in patients with established CV disease. Most Medicare beneficiaries seeking Wegovy for weight loss alone face coverage gaps under current law. State Medicaid coverage is expanding but remains variable. For detailed coverage guidance, see insuranceglp1.com/wegovy-coverage.html.

Can children take Wegovy?

Yes. Wegovy is FDA-approved for adolescents aged 12 and older with obesity, defined as BMI at or above the 95th percentile for age and sex. The dosing titration schedule is the same as for adults. Clinical trial data in adolescents showed approximately 16.1% body weight reduction over 68 weeks in the pediatric STEP TEENS trial. Parental or guardian consent and close pediatric provider supervision are required.

What is the difference between Wegovy and Ozempic?

Both contain semaglutide, but Wegovy (2.4 mg weekly) is FDA-approved for weight management, while Ozempic (0.5–2 mg weekly) is FDA-approved for type 2 diabetes. The higher Wegovy dose drives substantially greater weight loss: an average of 14.9% versus approximately 6% at Ozempic doses in T2D patients. Wegovy also has explicit BMI-based eligibility criteria on its label, whereas Ozempic's eligibility is based on diabetes diagnosis. Both are made by Novo Nordisk.

What is Wegovy HD and who qualifies?

Wegovy HD (7.2 mg semaglutide weekly) was approved by the FDA in March 2026 for adults who need additional weight reduction beyond what the standard 2.4 mg dose achieves. The eligibility criteria are the same: BMI ≥ 30, or BMI ≥ 27 with a comorbidity. Clinical data showed approximately 20.7% mean weight loss at 72 weeks, with approximately 1 in 3 patients achieving ≥25% weight loss. It is expected to become available on the U.S. market in 2026.

What happens to your weight when you stop Wegovy?

Most patients regain significant weight after stopping. The STEP 1 extension study found participants regained approximately two-thirds of prior weight loss within one year. A 2026 University of Cambridge model projected regain plateauing at about 75% of original loss, with roughly 25% sustained long-term. This underscores the need for a structured maintenance plan — including behavioral intervention, dietary support, resistance training, and potential continued low-dose therapy — before discontinuing Wegovy.
