Section 1

Why I Built This Calculator

In January 2025, I started Mounjaro (tirzepatide) under my physician’s supervision. By October 2025 I had lost fifty pounds in nine months. I was 58 years old, living in Udon Thani, Thailand, navigating GLP-1 access from outside the U.S. system.

What struck me was how much confusion existed at the very first step: do I even qualify? Patients were spending hours reading contradictory forum posts and marketing copy before they ever spoke to a provider. The BMI threshold for Ozempic, Wegovy, Mounjaro, and Zepbound is not complicated — but it is not clearly presented anywhere a patient would naturally look.

BMI ≥30 Typically qualifies
BMI 27–29.9 With comorbidity
<30 sec To get your result

I built this calculator to solve exactly that problem: give a patient a clear, FDA-guideline-grounded answer about whether their BMI falls within the range where Ozempic, Wegovy, Mounjaro, or Zepbound is typically indicated — in under thirty seconds, at no cost, with no email required.

“Patients deserve the same clear starting point a device rep would walk a physician through. That is what this tool provides.”

The result is a screening tool, not a diagnosis. It cannot account for your full medical history, your comorbidities, or your physician’s clinical judgment. It is the first step in a conversation with a provider — not a substitute for it.
